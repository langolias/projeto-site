Olhos D’Água Ecological Park

About the Park

Olhos D'Água Park is a public space for recreation and outdoor activities located in Asa Norte, Brasília, Federal District. As an ecological park, it was created to preserve nature and provide a pleasant area for people to enjoy green spaces and landscapes.

Created in 1994, the park covers 28 hectares and is one of the most visited places in the area. You can participate in various cultural events and physical activities there. The park preserves the typical vegetation of the cerrado and features a small lake. It is managed by the Federal District government (GDF).

A Bit More About

the Park's History...

The park was established thanks to local community efforts. To draw attention to its importance, the community organized actions such as marches along the Eixão do Lazer and, in 1993, built a Christmas tree made of trash collected from the park, created by artist Normando Rodrigues.

Law No. 556 established several goals for Olhos D'Água Park, including: protecting the springs and lake of SCLN 414/415; preserving and restoring vegetation around the lake; protecting the Paranoá Basin; promoting environmental observation and research programs; ensuring that visitors can enjoy the park without harming the environment; and developing environmental education activities.

The park offers a range of amenities. You can use the walking trails, playground, exercise circuit, and facilities such as restrooms, drinking fountains, and showers. There are also kiosks, running tracks, and benches for relaxation. Additionally, the park has recycling bins to help with waste separation

Park Features

The park is fully fenced and supervised by officers from the Environmental Battalion of the Military Police of the Federal District.

BIODIVERSITY

FLORA

The park features three main types of vegetation: cerrado grassland, mesophytic forest, and gallery forest. Common trees include barbatimão, cerrado jacaranda, faveiro, pau-jacaré, angico, and embaúba. However, in 2004, it was noted that some grass species like brachiaria, elephant grass, and fat grass had appeared due to human activities.

FAUNA

Um mico-estrela (Callithrix penicillata) em uma árvore do parque, em 2015

You can see various birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish in the park. Many of these animals help spread seeds and control insect populations. Because the park is close to Lake Paranoá and has preserved Mesophytic Forest areas, there are many bird species. Some abandoned domestic animals, such as rabbits, tortoises, and turtles, can also be found. The park's isolation from other cerrado areas limits animal migration, reducing the variety of species.

LESUIRE

ACTIVITIES

In addition to daily visits, Olhos D'Água Park hosts various activities, including cultural events, yoga classes, tai chi chuan, meditation sessions, and seed exchange fairs.

In 2010, the "Vô, Vó Malhar é no Parque" project was launched to encourage elderly people to use the Senior Fitness Academy (ATI) within the park. In 2012, participants, who could join for free, received guidance from physical education professionals.
